## ‚öñÔ∏è REGULATORY COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK

### Peru Data Protection Law (Law 29733) - Critical Compliance
**Political Opinions = Sensitive Personal Data**
- **Explicit Consent Required:** All political preference data collection requires specific, informed consent
- **Data Minimization:** Only collect data absolutely necessary for core functionality  
- **Purpose Limitation:** Data can only be used for stated educational/civic purposes
- **User Rights:** Right to access, correct, and delete political data at any time
- **Storage Limits:** Political data must be deleted after electoral cycles unless renewed consent

**Implementation Strategy:**
- Privacy-by-design architecture with granular consent management
- No political recommendations or ideological mapping features
- Focus on factual candidate information and voting logistics
- Regular compliance audits with Peru's ANPD (National Personal Data Protection Authority)

### JNE Electoral Regulation Compliance
**Polling & Predictions (Reglamento de Encuestas)**
- **Registration Required:** Any voting pattern predictions require JNE registration
- **Blackout Periods:** No predictions or recommendations during electoral silence periods
- **Technical Disclosures:** Methodology and sample size must be disclosed
- **Sanctions Risk:** Non-compliance can result in platform restrictions

**Safe Harbor Approach:**
- No voting predictions or electoral polling features
- Focus on factual candidate information from official sources
- Educational content about voting processes, not outcomes
- Clear disclaimers about non-predictive nature of platform

### Propaganda & Neutrality Rules (2025 Updates)
**Political Neutrality Requirements:**
- Platform must maintain strict political neutrality
- No endorsements, recommendations, or biased content
- Educational content must be balanced and factual
- Sponsored content must be clearly disclosed and non-partisan

**Content Strategy:**
- Factual candidate information only (public records, official positions)
- Educational materials about democratic processes
- Voting logistics and procedural information
- No personalized political recommendations or matching algorithms

1. **Competing with Free Official Platforms (JNE Voto Informado)**
   - **Impact:** Severe limitation on willingness to pay for similar features
   - **Mitigation:** Focus on superior UX, mobile-first design, educational content
   - **Contingency:** Pivot to pure educational/content model with sponsorships
   - **Probability:** 60%

   - **Mitigation:** Extended free trials, educational value over political guidance
   - **Contingency:** Advertising-supported model with educational content
   - **Probability:** 40%

3. **Regulatory Compliance Challenges (Peru Law 29733 + JNE Rules)**
   - **Impact:** Political opinions = sensitive personal data, strict consent requirements
   - **Mitigation:** Privacy-first design, legal compliance from day 1, no political recommendations
   - **Contingency:** Pure educational content, no personalized political features
   - **Probability:** 30%

4. **Severe Seasonal Revenue Volatility (¬±70% swings)**
   - **Mitigation:** University partnerships, municipal elections, educational content focus
   - **Contingency:** B2B institutional focus during off-cycles
   - **Probability:** 80%

5. **Payment Processing & Economics (15% store fees + 18% IGV tax)**

   - **Mitigation:** Multiple partnership tracks, direct relationships with key decision makers
